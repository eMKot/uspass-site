Privacy Policy

Effective: 16 April 2026 · Last updated: 16 April 2026

USPass ("the app") is developed by Mykola Kotliarenko. This privacy policy explains how the app handles your information.

Summary

USPass does not collect, store, or share any personal data. All your study progress, exam results, and preferences are stored locally on your device and never transmitted to us or any third party.

Data Controller

The data controller for the purposes of applicable privacy laws is:

Mykola Kotliarenko (sole trader)
Email: kotliarenkostudio@gmail.com

What We Do Not Collect

  • No personal information (name, email, phone number, address)
  • No location data
  • No device identifiers or fingerprints
  • No usage analytics or telemetry
  • No crash reports sent to third parties
  • No advertising identifiers
  • No use of the App Tracking Transparency framework or IDFA

Lawful Basis for Processing

USPass does not collect or process any personal data. All data generated through your use of the app is stored exclusively on your device and is not accessible to us.

Data Stored on Your Device

The app stores the following data locally on your device:

  • Study progress: which questions you have answered, your accuracy, and spaced repetition schedules
  • Exam history: scores, dates, and time taken for each practice exam
  • User preferences: appearance mode, test date, study reminder time, selected state
  • Custom flashcards: any flashcards you create within the app

This data is not accessible to us or any third party. It is not transmitted over the internet.

Data Retention

All app data is stored locally on your device for as long as the app is installed. We do not retain any data on servers or cloud systems. If you delete the app, the data in the app's sandboxed storage is removed by iOS. Note that data may persist in device backups (iCloud or local backups) managed by Apple, which are subject to Apple's Privacy Policy.

Third-Party Services

USPass does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking services.

The only external communication the app makes is through Apple's StoreKit framework for in-app purchases. Apple (not us) receives purchase transaction data including Apple ID and payment information. We receive only an anonymized purchase receipt confirming whether premium has been unlocked. This is handled entirely by Apple and is subject to Apple's Privacy Policy.

We do not transfer any personal data outside the United States. Apple may process purchase-related data internationally in accordance with their own privacy policy and data transfer mechanisms.

Children's Privacy (COPPA)

USPass is rated 4+ on the App Store and complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children under 13, or from anyone else. The app is designed with privacy by default — it does not profile users of any age, does not implement age estimation, does not display third-party advertising, and does not use data in ways that could be detrimental to children.

California Residents (CCPA / CPRA)

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), California residents have specific rights regarding their personal information. Because USPass does not collect, sell, share, or otherwise process any personal information:

  • We do not sell or share personal information, and we do not use personal information for targeted advertising.
  • Right to Know / Access: Not applicable — we hold no personal information about you.
  • Right to Delete: You can delete all locally-stored app data at any time using "Reset All Progress" in Settings, or by deleting the app.
  • Right to Correct: Not applicable — we hold no personal information about you.
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information: Not applicable — we collect no sensitive personal information.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We do not discriminate against any user for exercising their privacy rights.

You may contact us at any time at kotliarenkostudio@gmail.com to ask a question about these rights.

EU/EEA and UK Residents (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are a resident of the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and UK GDPR, including the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict processing of, and port your personal data, as well as the right to object to processing.

Since USPass does not collect, process, or store any personal data on our servers or systems, these rights are not exercisable against us — we hold nothing to access, correct, or delete.

If you believe your data protection rights have been infringed, you may lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority. For a list of EEA data protection authorities, see the European Data Protection Board members list. UK residents may contact the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

Your General Rights

You can delete all locally stored data at any time using the "Reset All Progress" option in the app's Settings, or by deleting the app from your device.

If you have concerns about the app's privacy practices, you may also contact the U.S. Federal Trade Commission at reportfraud.ftc.gov, or the California Attorney General's office at oag.ca.gov/privacy/privacy-complaint.

Changes to This Policy

If we update this privacy policy, the revised version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date. Since we do not collect contact information, we cannot notify you directly — please check this page if you have concerns.
